I was never bullied in school, although like many students, I experienced a few occasional insults. These moments didn’t affect me deeply, but they added to my natural shyness. I often preferred staying in the background rather than drawing attention to myself.

In Years 8 and 9, I struggled with public speaking because of anxiety and fear of embarrassment. I constantly worried that making mistakes during presentations or giving the wrong answer would lead to judgment from others. This fear made it difficult for me to express my ideas confidently in class.

By Year 10, I began to build my confidence after realising the importance of personal growth for my future. I felt more comfortable in my own skin thanks to the friends I had made along the way. Their support helped me face challenges with less fear and more courage.
